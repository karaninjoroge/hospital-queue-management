---

## Competitive Gap Analysis

**No existing solution addresses all four constraints simultaneously:**

1. **Works offline** — all software solutions assume reliable connectivity
2. **Works on feature phones** — all app-based solutions require smartphones
3. **Priced for public health** — enterprise solutions are unaffordable for county governments without grant funding
4. **Designed for African health facility workflow** — existing solutions assume retail or banking environments

**This gap defines our beachhead:** A queue management system built specifically for the constraints of African public health facilities — offline-first, SMS-native, and priced for institutional health buyers.

---

## Differentiation Strategy

We do not compete with Qmatic on features. We compete on fit.

Our advantage is that we start from the constraint (intermittent internet, feature phones, public health workflow) rather than retrofitting an enterprise product for a context it was not designed for.

The product that wins here is not the most feature-rich. It is the most reliable, the most context-appropriate, and the easiest to adopt with minimal training.
